Hospital Emergency Department Dashboard

Role: Business Intelligence Developer (Contract)
Subject: Operations, Access, and Throughput Analysis

1. Business Objectives & Operational Requirements

The hospital administration engaged me on a contract basis to develop a performance monitoring system for the Emergency Room. The objective was to allow leadership to monitor access, throughput, admissions, referrals, and patient experience across varying timeframes.

The dashboard was required to answer the following operational questions:

  • How many patients are being treated over time, and how does volume change month to month?
  • What is the average patient wait time, and does it remain within acceptable thresholds?
  • How effectively is the Emergency Room meeting its 30-minute access target?
  • What share of patients are being referred to other departments (identifying downstream bottlenecks)?
  • How do performance metrics behave when viewed monthly versus in aggregate?

2. Technical Implementation & Solution Strategy

The primary technical requirement was to design robust, reusable KPIs using DAX that would remain accurate under different time filters (monthly views vs. consolidated views) without data duplication.

2.2 KPI Design & DAX Logic

The core value of this project was the translation of operational requirements into DAX logic. I implemented the following measures to ensure consistent reporting:

Total Patient Volume (Baseline Demand):
Implemented as a distinct count of patient identifiers. This ensures accurate patient volume under all slicers and prevents double-counting when records span multiple dimensions.
Average Wait Time (Efficiency):
Calculated as a true average of patient wait times (in minutes). This measure was intentionally separated from threshold logic to allow for independent analysis of wait time trends regardless of targets.
Referral Rate (Flow Dependencies):
Calculated using conditional row counts. This KPI is filter-aware to reflect true referral behavior by period, enabling comparison of referral intensity across months to identify external bottlenecks.
Wait Time Status (Target Compliance):
The hospital explicitly requested visibility into 30-minute access compliance. I implemented conditional logic to classify patient wait times, differentiating between "Within Target" and "Missed Target" populations. This metric revealed operational failure rates that were previously hidden by simple averages.

2.3 Outcome

The final dashboard provided hospital stakeholders with a reliable, filter-safe KPI foundation. By moving beyond static reporting to decision-grade analytics, leadership gained the ability to compare month-level behavior against consolidated averages, allowing them to identify when apparent stability was masking underlying volatility.

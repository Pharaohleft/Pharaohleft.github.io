Healthcare ER Performance Dashboard

Business Intelligence Contract | Operations & Compliance Analytics

Project Context

The hospital engaged me on a Business Intelligence contract to build an Emergency Room performance dashboard that would allow leadership to monitor access, throughput, admissions, referrals, and patient experience across time.

Primary Requirement: Design robust, reusable KPIs using DAX that remain accurate under different time filters (monthly vs. consolidated views) without data duplication.

Business Questions Answered

  • How many patients are being treated over time, and how does volume change month to month?
  • What is the average patient wait time, and does it remain within acceptable thresholds?
  • What share of patients are being referred to other departments?
  • How effectively is the Emergency Room meeting its 30-minute access target?

Data & Schema Design

The hospital provided a structured dataset containing patient-level Emergency Room records. I structured this into a clean model to support the required questions.

Snapshot of the patient-level ER records.

To ensure performance comparisons remained accurate without manual recalculation, I utilized a star schema approach with dedicated measure tables.

The Data Model designed for filter-safe calculations.

KPI Design & DAX Logic

The core value of this project was the translation of operational requirements into DAX logic.

1. Total Patient Volume
Purpose: Establish baseline demand to track load over time and normalize other KPIs.
Approach: Distinct count of patient identifiers to prevent double-counting across dimensions.
2. Average Wait Time
Purpose: Monitor access efficiency and identify periods of operational strain.
Approach: Calculated as a true average (minutes), separated from threshold logic to allow independent trend analysis.
3. Referral Rate
Purpose: Understand downstream care demand and ER dependency on other departments.
Approach: Filter-aware conditional row counts to reflect true referral behavior by period.
4. Wait Time Status (Compliance Target)
Purpose: The hospital explicitly requested visibility into 30-minute access compliance.
Approach: Conditional logic to classify patients as "Within Target" vs "Missed Target." This revealed failure rates that average wait times often hid.

Outcome & Impact

The final dashboard provided hospital stakeholders with a reliable, filter-safe KPI foundation. By moving beyond static reporting to decision-grade analytics, leadership gained:

  • Clear visibility into Emergency Room access and throughput performance.
  • The ability to compare month-level behavior against consolidated averages to spot volatility.
  • A BI asset suitable for ongoing operational monitoring and executive review.

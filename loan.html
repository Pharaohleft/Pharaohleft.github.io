Bank Loan Analysis

DAX & Metrics Development | Business Intelligence Delivery

Project Background & Objective

As part of a contract engagement, the bank provided a historical loan dataset and requested a Power BI dashboard that could support portfolio monitoring, risk segmentation, and performance tracking across time.

To meet these requirements, I designed and implemented a custom DAX measures layer that translated raw transactional data into decision-ready KPIs, enabling consistent analysis across business views.

Objective of the DAX Layer

  • Standardize core portfolio KPIs across all dashboard views.
  • Enable time-based analysis (MTD, MoM, monthly trends).
  • Support "Good vs Bad" loan segmentation.
  • Allow credit-grade and risk slicing without duplicating logic.
  • Ensure metric consistency across summary, drill-down, and detail views.

Data & Schema Design

The foundation of accurate reporting was a clean data model. I structured the raw data into a star schema to optimize performance.

Snapshot of the raw loan data structure before transformation.

Throughout the development, I followed BI best practices:

  • Measure-driven model: Minimized calculated columns to improve performance.
  • Filter-context awareness: Ensured correct results across all slicers.
  • Single-definition KPIs: Reused base measures across multiple visuals.

The Data Model schema ensuring efficient filtering and one-to-many relationships.

DAX & Logic Implementation

1. KPI & Aggregation Measures

I created core aggregation measures to support portfolio-level visibility. These formed the single source of truth for Executive summary cards, Loan status tables, and Drill-downs.

  • Total Loan Applications
  • Total Funded Amount
  • Total Amount Received
  • Average Interest Rate
  • Average Debt-to-Income (DTI)

2. Time Intelligence & Trend Analysis

To support performance monitoring, I implemented DAX logic to calculate Month-to-Date (MTD) values and Month-over-Month (MoM) change percentages. This allows the business to identify acceleration patterns and compare current performance against recent periods without manual recalculation.

3. Risk Segmentation (Good vs Bad Loans)

To support credit risk analysis, I implemented logic to segment the portfolio. This allows risk teams to isolate loss-driving segments while preserving a consistent KPI framework:

  • Segment the portfolio into Good Loans and Bad Loans.
  • Compare funded amounts, recoveries, interest rates, and DTI across segments.
  • Display proportional contribution of bad loans to total exposure.

Strategic Business Questions

The DAX layer transformed the report from a simple display of data into a decision-support tool answering these 5 core questions:

1. Where is capital being deployed, and how much is recovered?
(Funded Amount vs Amount Received across the portfolio)
2. Which portion of the portfolio is driving financial loss?
(Bad Loans vs Good Loans in terms of funded capital & exposure)
3. How is portfolio risk trending over time?
(MTD / MoM changes in applications and recoveries)
4. Which loan structures carry the highest financial risk?
(Loan term, credit grade, interest rate vs recovery outcomes)
5. Which borrower profiles account for the most at-risk capital?
(Capital concentration by purpose, employment length, and home ownership)

Business Impact

The completed DAX framework enabled the bank to monitor loan performance in near real-time, compare portfolio health across risk segments, and make faster, data-driven decisions without manual analysis.

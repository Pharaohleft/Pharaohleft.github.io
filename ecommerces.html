E-Commerce Sales Performance Report

Role: Business Intelligence Developer
Subject: Profitability & Trend Analysis Implementation

1. Business Objectives & Key Questions

The ecommerce leadership team commissioned this project to address a lack of repeatable performance reporting. The primary challenge was not the absence of data, but the inability to answer strategic questions quickly and consistently.

The dashboard was required to answer the following specific business questions:

Executive Performance Questions

  • Are Year-to-Date (YTD) Sales up or down compared to the prior year?
  • Are YTD Profit and Profit Margins improving or deteriorating?
  • Is the performance change driven by Quantity (demand) or Profit Margin (efficiency)?

Segment & Category Questions

  • Which customer segment (Consumer, Corporate, or Home Office) is driving overall results?
  • Which product categories are growing or declining Year-over-Year within each segment?
  • Is performance explained by one category (concentration risk) or broad-based change?

Operational Questions

  • Which products contribute the most to sales (Top 5), and which contribute the least?
  • Which regions contribute the largest share of sales, and does the mix shift by segment?

2.1 Data Architecture

The solution was built on a transactional dataset of 115,746 rows. I transformed the raw data into a Star Schema, creating a dedicated Calendar Dimension to support time-intelligence functions.

2.2 Metric System Development (DAX)

I implemented a reusable DAX layer to standardize how performance is calculated. Below are the key technical specifications used to drive the insights:

Sales Volume Logic:
Calculated running totals for the current year and the prior year to derive the YoY% change. TOTALYTD(SUM('Ecommerce Data'[sales_per_order]), 'Calendar Table'[Date]) ([YTD Sales] - [PYTD Sales]) / [PYTD Sales]
Profitability Logic:
Implemented dynamic conditional logic to visualize trends using icons (Up/Down triangles) based on performance polarity. VAR positive_icon = UNICHAR(9650) VAR negative_icon = UNICHAR(9660) RETURN IF([YoY Profit] > 0, positive_icon, negative_icon)
Margin Analysis:
Calculated margin at the aggregate level to avoid average-of-averages errors. SUM('Ecommerce Data'[profit_per_order]) / SUM('Ecommerce Data'[sales_per_order])

2.3 Visual Layer Architecture

The visual design was mapped directly to the business questions:

  • KPI Row: Answers "Are we up or down?" using the DAX time-intelligence logic.
  • Category Table: Answers "What is driving the change?" by isolating specific category performance.
  • Top/Bottom Lists: Answers "Where is the concentration risk?" by highlighting the highest and lowest performing products.

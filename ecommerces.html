E-Commerce Sales Performance Report

Role: Business Intelligence Developer (Contract)
Subject: Profitability & Trend Analysis Implementation

1. Executive Summary & Project Context

The ecommerce leadership team commissioned the development of a centralized Power BI dashboard to standardize performance reporting. The primary operational challenge was not a lack of data, but the absence of a repeatable system for answering critical business questions. The organization required a solution to definitively determine if year-to-date performance was trending positively against prior years, isolate whether variances were driven by sales volume or profitability, and identify the specific customer segments driving these results.

The finalized solution provides an executive-facing view that maintains consistency across Customer Segments while supporting granular drill-downs into Category, Product, Geography, and Shipping dimensions.

2. Data Architecture & Modeling

The analysis is built upon a transactional dataset containing 115,746 rows and 39 columns. Key data points include Order Date, Sales, Profit Per Order, Order Quantity, Customer Segment, and Geography. To support time-intelligence functions, a dedicated Calendar Dimension was modeled and related to the transactional fact table.

Figure 1: Raw transactional data structure prior to modeling.

The schema design prioritizes a "Metric System First" approach. I constructed a reusable DAX KPI layer to ensure that time-based calculations such as Year-to-Date (YTD) and Prior-Year-to-Date (PYTD) remain consistent regardless of the slicer state. This prevents common reporting errors where definitions conflict across different business units.

Figure 2: Star Schema connecting the Calendar Dimension to Sales Transactions.

3. Technical Specifications (DAX)

The following section details the logic used to derive the core performance indicators. These measures were developed to support dynamic time-intelligence comparison.

3.1 Sales Volume Logic

The YTD Sales metric calculates the running total of sales for the current fiscal year. The PYTD Sales metric calculates the same figure for the exact corresponding period in the previous year to ensure a like-for-like comparison.

TOTALYTD(SUM('Ecommerce Data'[sales_per_order]), 'Calendar Table'[Date]) CALCULATE(SUM('Ecommerce Data'[sales_per_order]), DATESYTD(DATEADD('Calendar Table'[Date], -1, YEAR))) YoY % Change = ([YTD Sales] - [PYTD Sales]) / [PYTD Sales]

3.2 Profitability Indicators

Profit metrics follow the same time-intelligence pattern but include dynamic conditional logic for visualization. An icon generator was implemented to return a specific Unicode character (Up or Down triangle) based on the polarity of the Year-over-Year change.

VAR positive_icon = UNICHAR(9650) VAR negative_icon = UNICHAR(9660) RETURN IF([YoY Profit] > 0, positive_icon, negative_icon)

3.3 Margin Analysis

Profit Margin is calculated at the aggregate level to avoid average-of-averages errors. The formula divides total profit by total sales before applying time filters.

SUM('Ecommerce Data'[profit_per_order]) / SUM('Ecommerce Data'[sales_per_order])

4. Analytical Scope & Capabilities

The dashboard architecture was designed to answer specific strategic questions without requiring manual data manipulation. It enables Executive Leadership to assess whether sales, profit, and margins are improving or deteriorating relative to the prior year. It allows Segment Managers to determine which customer types (Consumer, Corporate, or Home Office) are accountable for overall results. Furthermore, it empowers Category Managers to identify concentration risks where a single product category may be masking broader performance issues.

This implementation successfully transitioned the client from static, ad-hoc reporting to a dynamic decision-support system.
